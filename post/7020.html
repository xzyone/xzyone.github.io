    Management

    The term management refers to the profess of coordinating and integrating work activities so that they are completed efficiently and effectively with and through other people. (Robbins et al., 2003, p. 5)

    Management is the process of designing and maintain an environment in which individuals, working together in groups, efficiently accomplish selected aims. (Wehrich & Koontz, 1993, p. 4)

    Importance of Management

    Research shows that competent management management is the key determinant to organizational success.

    • Decreased turnover
    • Increased profits
    • Greater sales
    • Greater stock value per employee

    Implications of Management Definitions

    • Management is concerned with effectiveness in achieving its goals.
    • Management is concerned with efficiency: maximizing the benefits for a given cost.

    Strategic Management

    Th art and science of formulating, implementing, and evaluating cross-functional decisions that enable an organization to achieve its objectives.

    Strategic management process

    Skills of Effective Managers to implement strategic process

    • Verbal communication (including listening)
    • Managing time and stress
    • Managing individual decisions
    • Recognizing, defining, and solving problems
    • Motivating and influencing others
    • Delegating
    • Setting Goals and articulating a vision
    • Self-awareness
    • Team building
    • Managing conflict

    Areas of management skills

    A high IQ alone does not guarantee success in life and work.

    Management skills can be improved with the proper balance of conceptual learning and behavioral practice.

    Strategy

    What “Strategic” means in different companies

    • Something important
    • Something that can’t be justified financially
    • Something with results that won’t be seen for 5 years
    • Something that only top management understand
    • Something vaguely defined

    Business strategy is header than racing strategy

    • The track ahead changes…
    • … and you have limited visibility
    • There is no end to a race.. a new one starts immediately
    • You have to redesign your car (organ)

    Organizations need to combine strategy, management and leadership to be successful

    Good leadership and management without good strategy risks disaster

    Company failures are often failures of strategy, though they get labeled “leadership” failures in the press.

    Classic definition of strategy: How we are going to create and sustain competitive advantage.

    Competitive advantage

    Competitive advantage: Superior performance relative to other competitors in the same industry or the industry average

    Key terms here - Superior and Relative

    Sustainable Competitive Advantage: Our performing over a prolonged period

    External Analysis

    Industry Structure, Competitive Forces, and Strategic Groups

    Importance of Analyzing the External Environment
    • Managers can mitigate threats.
    • Managers can leverage opportunities.
    • Gain understanding of potential impacts.
    • Understand the source.
    The way to analyzing the External Environment
    • The PESTEL Framework – useful to analyze the external environment
    • The Five Forces Model – useful to analyze the industry
    • Strategic Group Mapping – useful to see performance differences within the Same industry

    The PESTEL Framework

    • Groups environmental factors into six segments:
      • Political
      • Economic
      • Sociocultural
      • Technological
      • Ecological
      • Legal
    • These factors can create:
      • Opportunities
      • Threats
